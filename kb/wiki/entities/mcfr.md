  - Both the 2 MWth ARE and the 8 MWth MSRE, demonstrated extended successful MSR performance but under conditions less strenuous than those considered for a 1000 MWe or MCFR.

  - Fontenay-aux-Roses (CEA) and Pechiney Ugine Kuhlman (PUK) studied several high temperature MSR concepts [18,25,26], a Th/ $^{233}\mathrm{U}$ cycle in fast and thermal fluoride-salt reactors and an MCFR (U/Pu).
- [[../documents/cursory-first-look-at-the-molten-chloride-fast-reactor-as-an-alternative-to-the-conventional-batr-concept.md|Cursory First Look at the Molten Chloride Fast Reactor as an Alternative to the Conventional BATR Concept]] - April 1992
  - Goodman et al [11] proposed a molten-chloride fast reactor (MCFR) in 1952; Scatchard et al [12] reviewed the chemical problems involved.

  - In 1956, Bulmer et al, [13] designed and evaluated a 500 MWth MCFR, externally cooled.

  - In a plutonium-fueled MCFR, the high actinide density, the absence of core internals, and the very fast neutron spectrum can combine to raise BG up to 0.7.
- [[../documents/cursory-first-look-at-the-molten-chloride-fast-reactor-as-an-alternative-to-the-conventional-batr-concept.md|Cursory First Look at the Molten Chloride Fast Reactor as an Alternative to the Conventional BATR Concept]] - April 1992
  - MCFR(PU) studies [29] with a simplified reactor model showed that the temperature rise of the salt for a reactivity step of up to $1 should be less than 300°C; for 7 pumps failing out of 8, less than 230°C.
