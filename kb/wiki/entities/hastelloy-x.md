## Representative Claims

- [[../documents/ornl-2157.md|AEC RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT REPORT]] - September 10, 1956
  - Examination of a Hastelloy X thermal-convection loop operated with $\mathsf{NaF - ZrF_4 - UF_4}$ (50-46-4 mole %) at $1500^{\circ}F$ showed heavy intergranular subsurfacevoid formation to a depth of 35 mils.
- [[../documents/ornl-2157.md|AEC RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT REPORT]] - September 10, 1956
  - Tests were run to determine the effect of the fuel mixture $\mathrm{NaF - ZrF_4 - UF_4}$ (50-46-4 mole %) on the creep strength of Hastelloy X.
- [[../documents/ornl-2157.md|AEC RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT REPORT]] - September 10, 1956
  - The results indicate that Hastelloy X is subject to such severe corrosive attack by the fuel that a drastic reduction of its creep strength occurs at temperatures above $1500^{\circ}\mathrm{F}$ .
- [[../documents/ornl-2431.md|MOLTEN-SALT REACTOR PROGRAM]] - October 31, 1957
  - Inconel systems with high- and low-temperature regions at 1600 and $1200^{\circ}\mathrm{F}$ in which NaK-KF-LiF-UF $_4$ mixtures are circulated and Hastelloy-X systems operated at similar temperatures with the fuel mixture NaF-ZrF $_4$ -UF $_4$ belong to the class of systems in which chromium deposits occur.
