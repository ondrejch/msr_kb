## Summary

- In all of the candidate systems, it is presumed that gas stripping would be employed to remove noble-gas fission products and some volatile semi-noble metals (but not halogens).
- a. Most of the uranium (>95 percent) and volatile fission products are stripped in a continuous fluorinator. The $\mathrm{UF}_6$ so removed is decontaminated and promptly returned to the fuel salt and reduced to $\mathrm{UF}_4$ .
