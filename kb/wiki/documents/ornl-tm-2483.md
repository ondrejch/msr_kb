- Irradiation increases the yield strength of the base metal and welded samples at low temperatures. This damage anneals out as the test temperature is increased, and the welds and base metal show similar
- recovery. Thus, above $1200^{\circ}\mathrm{F}$ the yield strengths of base metal and welds annealed for 8 hr at $1600^{\circ}\mathrm{F}$ are equivalent and are unaffected by irradiation.
- Thus, the effects of welding on the strength parameters measured by standard tensile tests are relatively small and the most significant factor is the reduction in the fracture strain. The fracture strains observed for the welds involved in this study are summarized in Fig. 5.
