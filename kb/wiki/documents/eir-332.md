## Summary

- This report deals with a rather exotic "paper reactor" in which the fuel is in the form of molten chlorides.
- (d) Internally cooled fast breeder in which the cooling agent is the molten fertile material, the same as in the blanket zone. This reactor has a moderate breeding ratio of 1.38, a specific power of 0.22 GW(th)/m³ of core and very good inherent safety properties.
- In some special cases a hypothetical molten fluoride has been checked using $\mathsf{PuF}_3$ as the fissile, $\mathsf{UF}_3$ as the fertile and NaF and $\mathsf{ZrF}_4$ as the dilutents components. In this case one obtains a lower but still respectable breeding ratio of 1.5.
- The thermohydraulic studies show that even under the extreme operating conditions of very high neutron fluxes and high specific power, cooling is possible, in most cases by out-of-core cooling but also in one or two cases cooling internally in the core.
- Some molten salt reactor specific safety problems are discussed.
- The influence of fast neutrons on the chlorine, forming sulphur by the (n,p) reaction has been experimentally investigated and the results are reported briefly.
