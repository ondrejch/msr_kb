## Summary

- Scanning Electron Microscope Observations 21
- Experimental material of the three alloys was obtained in the form of 1/2-in.-thick plate. The vendor's and ORNL's chemical analyses are given in Table 1. The Hastelloy N was double vacuum melted and the Haynes alloys Nos. 25 and 188 were air melted and vacuum arc remelted.
- rate of decrease increased with increasing aging temperature. The lowest impact energies resulting from aging were from 3 to 8 ft-1b.
- in properties for aging temperatures of 800 and $900^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ is likely real since the same effect was noted at a test temperature of $300^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ (Fig. 8).
- The tensile properties of Haynes alloy No. 25 were changed markedly as a result of aging. Aging for 50 hr at $850^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ increased the yield stress
- No phase identification work was done on these samples, so we will only indicate the phases that are likely present on the basis of other studies. Carbides of the $\mathbf{M}_2\mathbf{C}$ and $\mathbf{M}_6\mathbf{C}$ type
