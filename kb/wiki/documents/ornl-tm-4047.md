## Summary

- Summary: General Comparison of Molten Salts with Lithium in Fusion Reactors 24
- The blanket of a fusion reactor serves to absorb and transfer the energy of the fusion reaction products, and to produce the tritium necessary to refuel the reactor. This report outlines how these two functions are performed by lithium-bearing molten salts.
- While fluorides are adequate heat-transfer agents and possess good radiation stability, neutronic transmutation of Be and F in the salt can lead to corrosion unless a redox buffer (analogous to $\mathrm{U}^{3+}/\mathrm{U}^{4+}$ in a fission reactor) is included in the melt.
