## Summary

- The heat exchangers are arranged so that, after the removal of shield plugs, the heads may be removed and leaky tubes may be plugged off by remotely manipulated equipment.
- Removal of fission products from the fuel stream is effected by discarding carrier salt every 8 years after fluorination to recover uranium. The spent salt is stored for future recovery when a complete molten-salt processing plant is available.
- Although the main control of the reactor consists in adjusting the concentration of fissile uranium in the fuel salt, auxiliary control is achieved by means of 6 cruciform control rods loaded with $\mathtt{B_4C}$ and clad with Hastelloy N.
- We believe that the design described here represents a molten-salt reactor plant which is feasible to build, will produce a significant amount of electrical power, and would be a major step toward a useful family of breeder reactors.
