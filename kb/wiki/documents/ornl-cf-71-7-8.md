## Summary

- 1. Measurements by Malinauskas and Savolainen have indicated that the solubility of hydrogen in molten salt is about $\frac{1}{3}$ the values used previously.
- 2. Only a small amount of lithium was found by chemical analyses of samples of insulation from the MSRE reactor furnace, so we conclude that the tritium in the reactor cell was produced in the fuel salt and diffused through the metal walls of the reactor system into the cell.
- 3. The rate of production of tritium in the fuel salt during the time that the tritium distribution was being measured is now estimated to be 54 curies per day.
- 4. Tritium was found in graphite removed from the reactor core in a quantity equivalent to a deposition rate of about 8 curies per day.
- Items 2-4 are discussed by P. N. Haubenreich in a memorandum now in preparation.
