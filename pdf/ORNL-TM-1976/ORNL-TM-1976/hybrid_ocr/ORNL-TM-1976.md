# A LITERATURE SURVEY OF THE FLUORIDES

# AND OXYFLUORIDES OF MOLYBDENUM

C. F. Weaver and H. A. Friedman

# INTRODUCTION

Molybdenum is one of the more important fission products with respect to the amount produced as well as its thermal neutron absorption cross section. Consequently the chemical behavior of molybdenum and its fluorides in molten salt reactor fuels which are in contact with graphite and Hastelloy is of interest. $^{1,2,3}$ A research program to determine the extent and rate of the pertinent reactions has recently been initiated. The necessary literature review of the fluorides and oxyfluorides of molybdenum is summarized in this report for the convenience of others.

# MoF3

Reported methods for the synthesis of $\mathbf{MoF}_3$ are:

1. $\mathsf{MoBr}_3 + 3\mathsf{HF} \rightarrow \mathsf{MoF}_3 + 3\mathsf{HBr}$ at $600^{\circ}$ in a Pt boat enclosed in a Cu tube. $^4$   
2. 2Mo + 6HF $\frac{225^0C}{24\mathrm{hrs}}$ 2MoF3 + 3H2.5   
3. MoF $^5$ +Mo $\rightarrow$ MoF $^3$ at $400^{\circ}C$ in a Ni capsule.6   
4. $\mathsf{MoF}_5 + \mathsf{SbF}_3 \rightarrow \mathsf{MoF}_3 + ?$ . The $\mathsf{SbF}_3$ was carried in a stream of A at 150 to $200^{\circ}\mathsf{C}$ .

The product produced by method (1) has different properties than that of methods (3) and (4). The authors of reference 6 stated that products similar to that obtained by method (1) were produced under hydrolyzing conditions. The properties of both materials are described below. Although it is possible for $\mathrm{MoF}_3$ to exist in two crystalline forms, it is more likely that the product described in reference 4 is an oxyfluoride.

Properties reported for "MoF₃" from method (1) are: non-hygroscopic, dark pink, shows no evidence of melting or subliming at $800^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ in the absence of air, is isostructural with $\mathrm{ReO}_3$ and $\mathrm{TaF}_3$ (cubic, space group $\mathbf{Pm}3\mathbf{m}$ , a = 3.8985 ± 0.005) at $<800^{\circ}$ , has a distorted structure at $>800^{\circ}$ and is reduced by $\mathrm{H}_{2}$ to Mo metal. The material produced by method (2) was found to be cubic by x-ray analysis and is probably the same as the product of method (1).

Properties reported for $\mathsf{MoF}_3$ from methods (3) and (4) are: variable color (ochre, light-green, gray, black, dark red, yellowish tan), stable to at least $900^{\circ}$ in Ni under its own pressure, stable to $500^{\circ}$ under vacuum, disproportionate above $600^{\circ}$ to form Mo metal and higher fluorides, density $4.64 \pm 0.07\mathrm{~g/cm}^3$ (measured), $4.50\mathrm{~g/cm}^3$ (x-ray) and $\mathsf{VF}_3$ type structure (space group R3c) determined by x-ray and neutron diffraction. Other workers (ref. 8) have confirmed that $\mathsf{MoF}_3$ has a bimolecular rhombohedral unit cell with the R3c space group. They have also shown that the compound is antiferromagnetic below $185^{\circ}\mathrm{K}$ . The trace of a neutron diffraction powder pattern taken at $4.2^{\circ}\mathrm{K}$ may be found in this reference.

# MOF 4

Reported methods for synthesis of $\mathsf{MoF}_4$ are:

1. $2\mathrm{Mo}(\mathrm{CO})_{6} + 3\mathrm{F}_{6}\xrightarrow{-75^{0}}2\mathrm{Mo}_{2}\mathrm{F}_{9} + 12\mathrm{CO}; \mathrm{Mo}_{2}\mathrm{F}_{9}\xrightarrow{170^{0}}\mathrm{MoF}_{4} +$ MoF5.   
2. Same as above except that the temperatures were $-65^0$ and $100^0$ , respectively. $^{10}$   
3. $\mathrm{Mo(CO)}_6 + \mathrm{MoF}_6 \rightarrow \mathrm{MoF}_5 + \mathrm{MoF}_4 + \mathrm{CO}$ ; volatile products removed by vacuum. 10,11   
4. $\mathrm{MoF}_5 > 150^{\circ}$ MoF $^6$ +MoF4.10,11

This compound has been described as light green, $^{9,11}$ non-volatile, $^{9,10,11}$ and immediately hydrolyzable with $\mathrm{H}_2\mathrm{O}$ .

# Mo2F9

An olive green solid of this composition has been reported, $^{9,10}$ but the authors of reference 9 suggested that it was a mixture rather than a single compound. It was prepared by the reactions: $4\mathrm{Mo}(\mathrm{CO})_{6} + 9\mathrm{F}_{2}\xrightarrow{-65 \text{ to } -75^{0}}2\mathrm{Mo}_{2}\mathrm{F}_{9}+$ 24CO and disproportionates by the reaction: $\mathrm{Mo}_{2}\mathrm{F}_{9}\xrightarrow{100-170^{0}}$ $\mathrm{MoF}_{4}+\mathrm{MoF}_{5}$ .

# MoF5

$\mathsf{MoF}_5$ is a yellow hygroscopic substance which melts to form a yellow viscous liquid.9,11 It fumes in air forming blue hydrolysis products, but is stable in air dried with $\mathsf{P}_2\mathsf{O}_5$ . The viscosity and high Trouton constant of $\mathsf{MoF}_5$ are explained by assuming self ionization: $2\mathsf{MoF}_5 \rightarrow \mathsf{MoF}_4^+ + \mathsf{MoF}_6^-$ .

This compound has been synthesized by:

1. Mo2F9 100-170 MoF4 + MoF5. 9,10   
2. $2\mathrm{MoF}_{6} + \mathrm{PF}_{3} \xrightarrow{\mathrm{amb.}} 2\mathrm{MoF}_{5} + \mathrm{PF}_{5}.$ 12-14   
3. Mo + F2 → MoF6 + "residue", "residue" distill MoF5. 6,11   
4. Mo(CO)6 + MoF6 25 MoF5 + MoF4 + CO. 10,11   
5. W(CO)6 + MoF6 → MoF5 + WF4 + CO. 11   
6. Mo(CO) + F2 -65 MoF 11 + (?)   
7. MoF $^6$ +Mo $\rightarrow$ MoF5.11   
8. $\mathbf{WF}_4 + 2\mathbf{MoF}_6 \xrightarrow{\mathbf{amb.}} 2\mathbf{MoF}_5 + \mathbf{WF}_6.$

$\mathsf{MoF}_5$ disproportionates irreversibly $(>150^{\circ})$ below its boiling point to form $\mathsf{MoF}_4$ and $\mathsf{MoF}_6$ . Its vapor pressure in the range 70.0 to $160^{\circ}$ is given by $\log \mathbf{P} = 8.58 - 2772 / \mathbf{T}$ . Table I provides a summary of the properties of $\mathsf{MoF}_5$ . $\mathsf{MoF}_5$ dissolves in $\mathsf{MoF}_6$ to form a yellow solution. It is monoclinic (space group C2m) with $a = 9.61 \pm 0.01\mathring{A}$ , $b = 14.22 \pm 0.02\mathring{A}$ , $c = 5.16 \pm 0.01\mathring{A}$ , and $\beta = 94^{\circ}21' \pm 20'$ . An electron density projection on the 001 plane and a table of interatomic distances may also be found in reference ll. $\mathsf{MoF}_5$ will reduce $\mathsf{UF}_6$ to $\mathsf{UF}_5$ in excess $\mathsf{UF}_6$ and to $\mathsf{UF}_4$ in excess $\mathsf{MoF}_5$ .

# MMoF6

The compounds $\mathsf{MMoF}_6^{15}$ ( $\mathbf{M} = \mathbf{Na}$ , K, Rb, Cs) were formed by: $2\mathsf{MoF}_6 + 2\mathsf{MI}^{-60^0}\mathsf{I}_2 + 2\mathsf{MMoF}_6$ and the impurities removed by exposure to vacuum at $200^{\circ}\mathbf{C}$ . All of these compounds form white crystals which are stable at $250^{\circ}\mathbf{C}$ , but attack glass above $250^{\circ}\mathbf{C}$ and turn blue in moist air. $\mathsf{KMoF}_6$ has a magnetic moment of 1.24 Bohr magnetons at $25^{\circ}\mathbf{C}$ , the low value being attributed to spin orbit coupling. The Na, Rb, and Cs compounds are cubic with a = 8.20, 5.11, and 5.29A, respectively. The K compound is tetragonal with a = 10.17 and c = 9.97A. $^{15}$ The Mo-F distance in $\mathsf{NaMoF}_6$ is 1.74 ± 0.03A. $^{11}$ . The Na compound has been further studied (ref. 16) and found to be face centered cubic, space group Fm3m ( $\mathsf{O}_h^5$ , No. 255). All of the interatomic distances are listed in this report.

# K2MoOF5

The compound $\mathsf{K}_2\mathsf{MoOF}_5$ has been reported<sup>15</sup> to be a readily hydrolyzed pale green solid.

# MoF6

Molybdenum hexafluoride has been synthesized by:

1. $\mathbf{F}_2 + \mathbf{Mo}^{60 - 400^0}$ MoF $6^{4,10,12,17 - 21}$ in Pt boat in Ni or Cu. F $2$ diluted with $\mathbf{N}_2$   
2. Mo + BrF3 → MoF6 + ?. 22   
3. Mo $^+$ ClF $\mathbf{\Phi}_3\to$ MoF $^+$ ? in Ni boat.20   
4. MoCl $^5$ + HF $\rightarrow$ MoF $^6$ + ?.20

The $\mathsf{MoF}_6$ is purified by trap to trap distillation over NaF.12,17,23 The reactivity of $\mathsf{MoF}_6$ with respect to fluorination has been described13,14 as: $\mathsf{CrF}_5 > \mathsf{UF}_6 > \mathsf{MoF}_6 > \mathsf{WF}_6$ . It forms a white solid18,24 and a colorless liquid.19,20,24,25 The colorless gas consists of octahedral molecules24-27 with $\mathsf{d}^2\mathsf{sp}^3$ hybrid bonds, has a bond strength of 105Kcal/mole13,28 and has a second virial coefficient of -923 cm³/mole.21

The Mo-F bond has a reported length of $1.83\text{\AA}$ , $29-31\text{\AA}$ , $1.840\text{\AA}$ , $27\text{\AA}$ , $1.830\text{\AA}$ , $32$ and a stretching force constant of $5.00$ , $30\text{\AA}$ , $5.13$ , $13\text{\AA}$ , $4.73$ , $14$ , $23$ , $5.087$ , $25$ , $5.080$ , $34$ , $4.9972$ , $35$ , $4.9875$ , $36$ ( $\textbf{x} 10^{-5}$ dynes/cm). The physical, structural, and thermal properties of $\mathsf{MoF}_6$ are summarized in Tables II-VII. Traces of the Raman, $19$ , $25$ , $27$ infrared, $19$ , $24$ , $27$ ultraviolet, $^{25}$ and nuclear magnetic resonance $^{37}$ spectra of $\mathsf{MoF}_6$ have been reported.

The values of $\mathbf{Cp}^0$ , $\mathbf{S}^0$ , $\mathbf{H}^0 - \mathbf{H}_0^0$ , $-(F^0 - H_0^0) / T$ for gaseous $\mathsf{MoF}_6$ in the ideal state have been calculated from the fundamental frequencies over the temperature range $50 - 1600^{\circ}\mathbf{K}$ and are tabulated in references 21, 24, 27, 31, and 38.

The values of Cp determined calorimetrically for solid and liquid $\mathsf{MoF}_6$ from 50 to $298.5^{\circ}\mathbf{K}$ are tabulated in reference 39. The enthalpy and entropy of gaseous $\mathsf{MoF}_6$ from 400 to $2000^{\circ}\mathbf{K}$ are tabulated $^{40}$ and summarized as $\mathbf{H}_{\mathbf{T}} - \mathbf{H}_{298.15} = 35.80\mathbf{T} + 0.59\mathbf{x}10^{-3}\mathbf{T}^{2} + 6.97\mathbf{x}10^{5}\mathbf{T}^{-1} - 13,064(298 - 2000^{\circ}\mathbf{K},$ gas) $^{40}$ and $\mathbf{Cp} = 35.80 + 1.18\mathbf{x}10^{-3}\mathbf{T} - 6.97\mathbf{x}10^{5}\mathbf{T}^{-2}$ . $^{40}$ The functions $\mathbf{Cp}^0$ , $\mathbf{S}^0$ , $\mathbf{H}^0 - \mathbf{H}_0^0$ , $-(F^0 - H_0^0)/T$ are also tabulated in reference 21 for solid $5-290.70^{\circ}\mathbf{K}$ , liquid $290.76 - 350^{\circ}\mathbf{K}$ , and gas $50 - 1000^{\circ}\mathbf{K}$ . The values for the solid and liquid are based on calorimetric data. Those for the gas are based on a combination of calorimetric and spectroscopic data.
